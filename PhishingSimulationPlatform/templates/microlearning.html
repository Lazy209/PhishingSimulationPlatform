You clicked a simulated phishing link. Here is what to look for next time.

1. Mismatched domain name

The email linked to {{ fake_domain }}, not the legitimate {{ real_domain }}. Always hover over links and verify the full URL before clicking.

2. Urgent or threatening language

Phrases like "Action required within 24 hours" and "account suspension" create panic so you act without thinking. Legitimate IT teams rarely demand immediate credential verification via email links.

3. Generic greeting

Real organizational emails usually address you by name. "Hello Employee" or vague salutations are a common signal of mass phishing campaigns.

4. Unexpected credential request

Microsoft, Google, and banks will not ask you to re-enter your password through a link embedded in an unsolicited email. Use official apps or type the known URL directly in your browser.

5. Sender address vs. display name

Attackers spoof display names like "Microsoft 365 Security." Always check the actual sender email domain — it often reveals the deception immediately.
